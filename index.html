Michelangelo Buonarroti

Michelangelo Buonarroti, born in 1475 in Caprese, Italy, was one of the most influential artists of the Italian Renaissance. Renowned as a sculptor, painter, architect, and poet, Michelangelo's work epitomizes the ideals of the Renaissance era. He is best known for his masterpieces such as the statue of David, the Sistine Chapel ceiling in Vatican City, and the dome of St. Peter's Basilica in Rome. Michelangelo's artistic brilliance extended beyond his technical skill; he possessed a profound understanding of human anatomy and a unique ability to capture emotion and movement in his work. His legacy continues to inspire artists and admirers worldwide, cementing his status as one of the greatest artists in history.
